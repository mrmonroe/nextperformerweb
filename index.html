Competition Analysis

No dominant app exists for open mic scheduling. Current solutions include:

  • Meetup - Strong event discovery, no slot signup
  • Eventbrite - Strong ticketing, no slot management
  • Facebook - Community-driven, limited functionality
  • OpenMicX - Streaming focus, weak discovery

Business Model

Freemium SaaS Platform with dual revenue streams:

Hosts

  • Free Tier: 1 event, 1 venue, 1 date, signup sheet only
  • Pro Tier ($10/mo): Multiple events/venues, lineup timers, analytics

Venues

  • $50/mo subscription for featured placement and sponsorships

Performers

  • Always Free - Growth lever and virality driver

Monetization Strategy

Break-Even Analysis

Break-Even Point: Only 8 Pro Hosts OR 2 Venues needed to cover $71.25/month operating costs.

Revenue Streams

  • Host Subscriptions: $10/month Pro tier
  • Venue Sponsorships: $50/month featured placement
  • Future: Premium performer features, local advertising

Glossary

  • ARR (Annual Recurring Revenue): Revenue earned from subscriptions in a year
  • ARPU (Average Revenue Per User): Average revenue generated per user
  • CAC (Customer Acquisition Cost): Average cost to acquire a paying customer
  • Churn: Percentage of customers that cancel in a given period
  • Freemium: Basic product free, advanced features require payment
  • KPI (Key Performance Indicator): Metrics used to measure success
  • LTV (Lifetime Value): Total expected revenue from a customer
  • MRR (Monthly Recurring Revenue): Revenue from subscriptions per month
  • MVP (Minimum Viable Product): Smallest version that delivers value
  • SaaS (Software-as-a-Service): Cloud-based software via subscription
  • SAM (Serviceable Available Market): Portion of market you can realistically serve
  • SOM (Serviceable Obtainable Market): Subset of SAM you can realistically capture
  • TAM (Total Addressable Market): Entire potential market if adoption was 100%
