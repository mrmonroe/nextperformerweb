## 1. Executive Summary
Next Performer is a **SaaS platform** designed to connect **hosts, venues, and performers** for open mic events.  
- **Problem**: Signups are chaotic (clipboards, first-come lines, Facebook posts). Performers struggle to find events; hosts struggle to manage lineups.  
- **Solution**: A web + mobile app for event discovery, digital signups, and lineup management.  
- **Microniche**: Acoustic musicians & comedians in Florida metro areas (Jacksonville, Orlando, Miami) with expansion potential nationwide.  
- **Approach**: Lean solo-founder SaaS with minimal operating costs, sustainable from early adoption.

## 6. Monetization Strategy

### Hosts
- **Free Tier**:  
  - 1 event, 1 venue, 1 date.  
  - Signup sheet only.  
- **Pro Tier ($10/mo)**:  
  - Multiple events, multiple venues.  
  - Lineup timers & slot mgmt.  
  - Collect performer contact info.  
  - Analytics & advanced tools.  

### Venues
- **$50/mo** subscription for featured placement, promotions, and sponsorships.  

### Performers
- Always free (growth lever, virality).

## 10. Glossary

- **ARR (Annual Recurring Revenue)** ‚Äì Revenue earned from subscriptions in a year (MRR √ó 12).  
- **ARPU (Average Revenue Per User)** ‚Äì Average revenue generated per user per month or year.  
- **CAC (Customer Acquisition Cost)** ‚Äì Average cost to acquire a paying customer (ads, outreach, sales).  
- **Churn** ‚Äì Percentage of customers that cancel or stop using the service in a given period.  
- **Freemium** ‚Äì A pricing model where the basic product is free, but advanced features require payment.  
- **KPI (Key Performance Indicator)** ‚Äì Metrics used to measure success (e.g., # of hosts onboarded).  
- **LTV (Lifetime Value)** ‚Äì Total expected revenue from a customer over their entire relationship.  
- **MRR (Monthly Recurring Revenue)** ‚Äì Revenue from subscriptions per month.  
- **MVP (Minimum Viable Product)** ‚Äì The smallest version of the product that delivers value to users.  
- **SaaS (Software-as-a-Service)** ‚Äì Cloud-based software accessed via subscription instead of one-time purchase.  
- **SAM (Serviceable Available Market)** ‚Äì Portion of the market you can realistically serve.  
- **SOM (Serviceable Obtainable Market)** ‚Äì Subset of SAM you can realistically capture (e.g., initial pilot cities).  
- **TAM (Total Addressable Market)** ‚Äì Entire potential market for a product/service if adoption was 100%.  
- **Unit Economics** ‚Äì Per-customer financials, including revenue, costs, CAC, and LTV.
